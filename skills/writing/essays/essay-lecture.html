ESSAY DROP
01 / 12
B2 English Writing
WHAT IS
AN ESSAY?
A formal, structured piece of writing where you discuss a topic — arguing, agreeing, disagreeing, listing pros & cons, or expressing a personal view.
NOT A STORY. NOT A CHAT. A DISCUSSION.
The Blueprint
3 PARTS
1
Introduction
Hook the reader. State your aim.
2
Body Paragraphs
Build your argument. One idea per paragraph.
3
Conclusion
Summarise. Give your final verdict.
01
Paragraph 1
THE INTRO
Two jobs only:
① Hook sentence  +  ② Thesis sentence
Sentence 1 of your introduction
THE HOOK 🎣
Background · Trend · Fact · Quote — choose one
Nowadays, [topic] is becoming increasingly common in modern society.
In recent years, there has been a growing debate about [topic].
It is widely believed that [topic] has changed the way we live.
Many people argue that [topic] is one of the most important issues today.
There is an ongoing debate about whether [topic] does more good than harm.
It is impossible to deny that [topic] plays a significant role in our lives.
Over the past few decades, [topic] has become a much-discussed topic.
Sentence 2 of your introduction
THESIS
Sets the aim of the essay.
Tells the reader what is coming.
This essay will discuss the main advantages and disadvantages of [topic].
In this essay, I will examine both sides of this argument before giving my opinion.
The aim of this essay is to consider the causes of [topic] and suggest solutions.
02
The Engine Room
BODY
PARAGRAPH
Every body paragraph has two parts:
Make a Claim  →  Support the Claim
Part 1 of the Body Paragraph
MAKE THE CLAIM
3 moves. Always in this order.
①
Listing Word
Firstly,  /  To begin with,  /  Secondly,
Organises your argument. Tells the reader where you are.
②
Opinion Phrase
it seems to me that  /  I believe  /  in my view
Establishes YOUR position immediately.
③
The Actual Claim
the key advantage of [X] is that…
The specific point you are making. One idea only.
Watch it build
① LISTING WORD
Firstly, ...
Firstly, it seems to me that ...
Firstly, it seems to me that the key advantage of social media is that it connects people.
Three moves. One sentence. Maximum marks.
Part 2 of the Body Paragraph
SUPPORT
THE CLAIM
After the claim, you must develop it.
Use these linking signals to guide the reader:
Cohesion & Flow — the magic words
6 MOVES 💥
REASONThis is because…
EXPLAINIn other words…
EXAMPLEFor instance…
RESULTAs a result…
COMPARESimilarly…
CONTRASTHowever…
You don't need all 6. Pick 2 or 3. Keep it flowing.

THE FULL BODY PARAGRAPH
①②③
CLAIM
"Firstly, it seems to me that the key advantage of social media is that it connects people."
WHY
REASON
"This is because platforms allow people from different countries to communicate instantly."
EG
EXAMPLE
"For instance, many students use apps like WhatsApp to collaborate on projects across borders."
∴
RESULT
"As a result, social media has made the world feel significantly smaller and more connected."
03
The Final Move
CONCLUSION
Don't introduce new ideas. Summarise + give your final verdict.
To sum up, [topic] has both advantages and disadvantages.
Overall, I believe that the benefits of [topic] outweigh the drawbacks.
All things considered, [topic] can be positive if managed properly.
In conclusion, I firmly believe that [topic] is beneficial for society.
